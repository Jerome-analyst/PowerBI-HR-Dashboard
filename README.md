# HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI Case Study)

## Overview  

This project presents an **HR Analytics Dashboard** developed using Microsoft Power BI. The dashboard provides an interactive analysis of employee data, focusing on attrition, workforce distribution, compensation, and employee satisfaction.

The goal of this project is to transform raw HR data into meaningful insights that support **data-driven decision-making** in workforce management and employee retention.

## Context  

Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations, leading to increased costs in recruitment, onboarding, and lost productivity. Understanding the factors that influence employee turnover is essential for improving retention and maintaining organizational performance.

This dashboard was developed to provide a comprehensive view of workforce data, enabling HR teams to analyze patterns in employee behavior, compensation, and job satisfaction.

---

## Business Questions  

The dashboard aims to answer the following key questions:

- What is the overall attrition rate of the organization?  
- Which departments and job roles experience the highest turnover?  
- How do overtime, business travel, and commute distance affect attrition?  
- Are employees satisfied with their compensation and work-life balance?  
- How do salary and promotion timelines influence employee retention?  

---

## Insights  

### Workforce Overview  
- Total Employees: 1,470  
- Attrition Rate: 16.1%  
- Majority of employees are within the 25–50 age group  
- Male employees represent a larger portion of the workforce  

### Attrition Drivers  
- Sales department has the highest attrition rate (20.6%)  
- Sales Representatives show the highest turnover (~39.8%)  
- Employees working overtime have significantly higher attrition (53.6%)  
- Frequent business travel increases the likelihood of resignation  

### Employee Behavior and Satisfaction  
- Lower job satisfaction is strongly linked to higher attrition  
- Work-life balance score (2.76 / 4) indicates moderate satisfaction  
- Average commute distance (9.2 km) may influence employee decisions  

### Compensation and Career Growth  
- Average monthly income is $6,503  
- Lower job levels (L1–L2) receive significantly lower salaries  
- Promotion timelines are longer in Sales (~2.4 years)  

---

## Recommendations  

- Reduce overtime by improving workload distribution and staffing  
- Implement targeted retention strategies for high-risk roles, especially in Sales  
- Enhance work-life balance through flexible work arrangements and wellness programs  
- Review and adjust compensation structure for lower job levels  
- Improve career development by shortening promotion timelines and defining clear growth paths  

---

## Possible Impact  

- Reduction in attrition rate from 16% to approximately 12%  
- Decrease in recruitment and training costs  
- Improved employee satisfaction and engagement  
- Higher retention of skilled and high-performing employees  
- More effective and data-driven HR decision-making
